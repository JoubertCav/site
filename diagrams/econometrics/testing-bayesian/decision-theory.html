Decision theory

A decision rule maps observations into actions. Its quality is not one number until we decide how to aggregate loss across states: risk functions compare rules state by state, Bayes risk averages with a prior, minimax risk protects against the worst state, and admissibility removes dominated rules.

Interpretation
Risk function.
A rule is evaluated state by state. In a two-state problem, every rule becomes a point in the risk plane.
Bayes risk.
A prior π turns the risk vector into one weighted average. Graphically, Bayes chooses the lowest supporting line.
Minimax risk.
Minimax minimizes the worst coordinate. Graphically, it finds the smallest square touching the risk set.
Admissibility.
A rule is inadmissible if another rule lies weakly southwest and strictly southwest in at least one coordinate.
