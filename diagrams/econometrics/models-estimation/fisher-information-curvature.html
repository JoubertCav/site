Score, Fisher information, and the Cramér–Rao bound

A single geometric chain: the law changes with the parameter, the score records the relative parameter derivative, Fisher information averages the squared score under the law, the expected log-likelihood inherits the same curvature, and the Cramér–Rao lower bound converts that curvature into a minimum variance scale.
Law derivative
∂θ fθ(x) how the probability law changes when θ moves
Score
Uθ(x)=∂θ\log fθ(x)=\frac{∂θfθ(x)}{fθ(x)} relative parameter sensitivity of the law
Information
I₁(θ)=Eθ[Uθ(X)^2] = -Eθθ(X)] variance of the score = expected negative curvature
Cramér–Rao
Var(T)\ge \frac{1}{nI₁(θ)} for unbiased regular estimators T

Interpretation
Score.
For fixed θ, the map x ↦ Uθ(x) tells how one observation pushes the parameter locally in log units.
Fisher information.
I1(θ) is not a new object: it is the law-weighted squared score, hence the average local sensitivity of the law to θ.
Curvature.
Under regularity, the same I1(θ) is the downward curvature of the expected log-likelihood at the truth.
CRLB.
Unbiased regular estimators obey Var(T) ≥ 1/(nI1(θ)). For biased regular estimators, the derivative of the bias changes the variance bound, and MSE adds bias².
