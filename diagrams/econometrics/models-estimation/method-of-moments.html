Method of moments

Match sample moments with population moments. The estimator is the parameter value whose theoretical moments reproduce the empirical ones. Geometrically: a parameter point maps into moment space; the sample determines a moment target; method of moments inverts that map.
Population moments
m(θ)=E[g(X,θ)]theoretical restrictions implied by the model
Sample moments
\(\bar g_n = \frac1n\sum_i g(X_i)\)empirical moment vector from the data
MoM estimator
m(\hat θ_n)=\bar g_nmatch theoretical and empirical moments

Interpretation
Core idea.
Replace population moments by sample moments, then solve the theoretical moment equations for the parameters.
Just-identified case.
The number of equations equals the number of unknown parameters, so the empirical moment target pins down one estimate.
Consistency.
When sample moments converge to population moments and the map is identifiable, the inverse image converges to the true parameter.
CLT logic.
The sample moment vector obeys a CLT; linearizing the inverse map transfers that normality to the estimator.
