Likelihood, score, and Hessian

Likelihood is a compatibility surface over parameters. The log-likelihood peak is the MLE, the score is the slope, the Hessian is the curvature, and likelihood ratios measure loss from the peak.
Likelihood
L(θ;x)=∏ f(xᵢ;θ) compatibility of parameter θ with observed data
Log-likelihood
ℓ(θ)=logL(θ;x) same maximum, cleaner geometry
Score equation
U(θ)=ℓ′(θ),   U(θ̂)=0 at an interior maximum the tangent is flat
Curvature and LR
J(θ̂)=−ℓ″(θ̂) LR(θ)=2[ℓ(θ̂)−ℓ(θ)]

Interpretation
Likelihood.
The data are fixed; θ moves. Higher likelihood means the model at θ makes the observed sample more compatible.
Score.
The score is the derivative of the log-likelihood. Positive score means move right; negative score means move left.
Hessian.
At the peak, \(J(θ̂)=-ℓ''(θ̂)\) measures sharpness. Sharper peak means more local information.
Likelihood ratio.
\(LR(θ)\) is twice the vertical drop from the maximum, giving a natural evidence distance.
