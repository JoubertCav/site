Maximum likelihood estimation

MLE is a map from the whole sample to the parameter value that makes the observed data most compatible with the model. In large samples, the random maximizer concentrates around the true parameter or the pseudo-true parameter, becomes approximately normal, and can be efficient under correct specification.

What to notice
Compact exam-style summary
Definition.
\(\hat θ_n\in\arg\max_\theta \ell_n(\theta)\), where \(\ell_n(\theta)=\sum_i\log f_\theta(X_i)\).
Invariance.
For a function \(g\), the MLE of \(g(θ)\) is \(g(\hat θ_n)\), with care if \(g\) is many-to-one.
Consistency.
Under identification and regularity, \(\hat θ_n\to θ_0\). Under misspecification, \(\hat θ_n\to θ^*\).
QMLE.
When the model is wrong, use sandwich variance \(A^{-1}BA^{-1}/n\), not the inverse Hessian alone.
