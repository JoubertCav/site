Estimators as random variables

Combine the two ideas: a random variable is a measurable map, and an estimator is a random variable whose input is the whole sample. Geometry: samples live in \(\Omega^n\), estimates live in the parameter line, and sampling distributions are pushforwards of repeated samples.

Interpretation
Estimator as map.
A sample \(ω_n=(X_1,\dots,X_n)\) is one point in sample space. The estimator sends that whole point to one number.
Sampling distribution.
The histogram is the pushforward law: probabilities of estimates are probabilities of inverse-image sample sets.
Bias and variance.
Bias is center shift; variance is spread of the pushforward law; MSE combines both.
Consistency.
As \(n\) grows, a consistent estimator collapses the pushforward law toward a point mass at θ.
What to notice
Estimator = random variable on sample space · sampling distribution = pushforward measure
