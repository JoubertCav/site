Exponential family

What equation is being solved?

The law is a theorem about constraints.
Entropy objective
\[H(p)=-\sum_{x\in S}p(x)\log p(x)\]

High entropy means the mass is spread out. Concentrating mass lowers entropy.

Constrained problem
\[\max_p H(p)\quad\text{s.t.}\quad \sum_x p(x)=1,\; \mathbb E[X]=m,\;\operatorname{Var}(X)=v\]

You only get the distribution after declaring which information is admissible as a constraint.

Exponential-family emergence
\[p_\eta(x)=\exp\{\eta_1x+\eta_2x^2-A(\eta)\}\]

The natural parameter \(\eta\) is chosen so the required moments match the imposed constraints.

Geometry of the constraint set

In a simplex, MaxEnt chooses the highest contour of entropy touching the feasible set.
Three-outcome simplex
\[p_0+p_1+p_2=1,\qquad \mathbb E[X]=p_1+2p_2=m\]

The mean constraint is a straight line. The MaxEnt point is where entropy is largest along that line.

The conceptual algorithm

A compact map from assumptions to distribution.
1
Declare support

Say where the variable can live. This is already a strong assumption: finite, positive, bounded, continuous, discrete, etc.

2
Declare constraints

State the moments or probabilities you actually know: mean, variance, tail bound, event probability, and so on.

3
Maximize entropy

Choose the least additional structure consistent with those declarations. The resulting law is the transparent consequence of the assumptions.

Built for studying: entropy is not a magic distribution generator; it is a discipline for making assumptions explicit.
