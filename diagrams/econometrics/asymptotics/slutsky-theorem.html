Slutsky's theorem

Slutsky makes inference feasible: replace unknown constants in an asymptotic distribution by consistent estimates, and the limiting law stays the same.
Core theorem
Xn ⇒ X,   Yn → c then sums, products, and quotients inherit limits
Feasible statistic
Tn = Xn / Rn where \(R_n=\widehat\sigma_n/\sigma \to_p 1\)
Mean inference
√n( X̄n−μ ) / Sn ⇒ N(0,1) unknown σ replaced by sample standard deviation
Confidence interval
X̄n ± z1−α/2Sn/√n feasible asymptotic inference

Interpretation
Infeasible limit.
If \(Z_n=\sqrt n(\hat\theta_n-\theta)/\sigma\Rightarrow Z\), the limit often contains unknown \(\sigma\).
Consistent replacement.
If \(R_n=\widehat\sigma_n/\sigma\to_p1\), then \(Z_n/R_n\Rightarrow Z\).
Feasible inference.
The limiting distribution becomes usable because all unknown pieces are estimated.
Failure mode.
If the replacement converges to the wrong constant, the feasible statistic has the wrong scale.
