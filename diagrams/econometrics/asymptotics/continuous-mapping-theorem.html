Continuous mapping theorem

A continuous function carries convergent random sequences into convergent transformed sequences. Geometrically: mass moves through a stable map without tearing at the limiting support.
CMT
Xn ⇒ X,   g continuous g(Xn) ⇒ g(X)

Interpretation
CMT.
If \(X_n\Rightarrow X\) and \(g\) is continuous, then \(g(X_n)\Rightarrow g(X)\).
Why continuity?
Small errors in \(X_n\) must become small errors after applying \(g\).
Discontinuities.
A discontinuity is harmless only if the limiting random variable avoids it with probability one.
Feasible inference.
CMT lets asymptotic approximations survive transformations such as squares, exponentials, and absolute values.
