Weak convergence and distributional limits

Weak convergence means the laws approach: CDFs converge only at continuity points, smooth test expectations converge, and probabilities of sets obey Portmanteau inequalities.
CDF criterion
Xn ⇒ X ⇔ Fn(x) → F(x) only at continuity points of \(F\)
Law, not paths
𝓛(Xn) ⇒ 𝓛(X) no common-space closeness required
Test functions
E[g(Xn)] → E[g(X)] for bounded continuous \(g\)
Portmanteau
limsup Pn(C) ≤ P(C) closed \(C\); reverse inequality for open sets

Interpretation
CDFs.
Weak convergence checks \(F_n(x)\to F(x)\) only where \(F\) is continuous.
Atoms.
At jumps, \(F_n(x)\) may fail to converge to \(F(x)\) without violating weak convergence.
Test functions.
Bounded continuous functions smooth out small jumps and moving atoms.
Approximation.
Asymptotic laws replace complicated finite-\(n\) laws by a simpler limiting law.
