Lebesgue integration from simple functions

Build the integral from measurable blocks. A simple function assigns one height to each measurable piece; the integral is the sum of height × measure. General integrals arise by approximation and decomposition.

What to notice
Lebesgue integration begins with measurable pieces in Ω, not with intervals on the vertical axis. The measure of each piece decides its weight.

Interpretation
A simple function compresses f into finitely many heights. The integral adds these heights with weights μ(Aᵢ).
Simple functions.
s = ∑aᵢ1Aᵢ. Geometry: finitely many horizontal slabs over measurable pieces.
Nonnegative integral.
Approximate from below and take the supremum of simple integrals.
Signed integral.
Write f=f⁺−f⁻. The integral exists when ∫f⁺dμ and ∫f⁻dμ are not both infinite.
Expectation.
A random variable is a measurable function; its mean is its Lebesgue integral under P.
Exam-style summary
For s=∑aᵢ1Aᵢ, define ∫s dμ=∑aᵢμ(Aᵢ). For f≥0, define ∫f dμ as the supremum over simple 0≤s≤f. For signed f, set f⁺=max(f,0), f⁻=max(−f,0), and ∫f=∫f⁺−∫f⁻ when ∫|f|dμ<∞.
