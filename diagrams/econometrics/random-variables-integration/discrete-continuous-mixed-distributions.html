Discrete, continuous, and mixed distributions

A probability law may live as atoms, as density spread over intervals, or as both. Mixed economic outcomes often have a point mass at zero and a continuous positive tail.

Interpretation
A mixed law has two geometries at once: a positive jump at one point and continuous probability spread across intervals.
Discrete.
Probability sits on points. CDF moves by jumps.
Continuous.
Probability is area under density. Single points have zero probability.
Mixed.
Some mass is atomic; the rest is absolutely continuous.
Economic example.
Income can have P(Y=0)>0 and a continuous distribution over Y>0.
What to notice
A density height is not probability. Probability is area. But an atom is probability at a point, visible as a jump in the CDF.
