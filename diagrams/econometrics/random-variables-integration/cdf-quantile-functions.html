CDFs and quantile functions

A distribution function accumulates mass from left to right. A quantile function reverses that geometry: start with a probability level u and find the first point where accumulated mass reaches u.
Distribution function
F(x)=P(X≤x)
Right-continuity
F(x)=F(x+)
jumps include their mass
Generalized inverse
Q(u)=inf{x:F(x)≥u}
Quantile representation
U∼Unif(0,1) ⇒ Q(U)∼F

Interpretation
F(x) is accumulated probability. Q(u) is the smallest x whose accumulated mass reaches u.
CDF.
Nondecreasing, right-continuous, with limits 0 and 1.
Jump.
A vertical jump is an atom: P(X=x)=F(x)-F(x−).
Quantile.
Use infimum, not ordinary inverse, because F may jump or be flat.
Order stats.
X(k)≈Q(k/(n+1)): sample ranks are probability levels.
What to notice
For continuous strictly increasing F, the quantile is the usual inverse. For jumps and flats, the generalized inverse keeps the construction well-defined.
