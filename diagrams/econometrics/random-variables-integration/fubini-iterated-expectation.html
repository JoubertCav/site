Fubini and iterated expectation

Product integration is geometry on a rectangle: integrate by summing vertical slices, or by summing horizontal slices. Fubini says both orders agree when the integrand is integrable; Tonelli says nonnegative mass is safe even before integrability is known.

Interpretation
Fubini converts a two-dimensional integral into two one-dimensional integrations. The theorem is a permission rule for changing the order of summation of infinitesimal rectangles.
Product integral.
Area is computed over the rectangle by adding contributions from small cells.
Fubini.
If ∫|h|d(μ×ν)<∞, both iterated integrals exist and agree.
Tonelli.
If h≥0, both orders are safe, possibly with value +∞.
Iterated expectation.
E[h(X,Y)] = E[E[h(X,Y)|X]], a slice-average followed by an outer average.
What to notice
The inner integral compresses a whole slice into one number. The outer integral then averages those slice-values.
