Expectation can be computed on the hidden sample space Ω, or after transporting probability through X onto the real line. LOTUS says: to integrate a function of X, integrate that function against the law of X.

Interpretation
LOTUS avoids reconstructing the original sample space. Once the law μX is known, any expectation of g(X) can be computed on the real line.
Sample-space view.
Average the realized numbers g(X(ω)) over ω∈Ω.
Law view.
Push P through X, then average g(x) with respect to μX.
Change of variables.
Y=g(X) has law μY(A)=μX(g−1A).
Unconscious statistician.
You do not need the density of Y to compute E[g(X)].
