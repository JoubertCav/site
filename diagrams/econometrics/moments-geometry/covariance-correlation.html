Covariance and correlation

Covariance is an inner product between centered random variables. Correlation is its normalized cosine. Partial correlation removes the component explained by a third variable.
Covariance
Cov(X,Y)=E[(X−μX)(Y−μY)]
centered inner product
Correlation
ρXY = Cov(X,Y)/(σXσY)
cosine in L²
PSD matrix
Σ = E[(X−μ)(X−μ)′]
a′Σa = Var(a′X) ≥ 0
Partial correlation
ρXY·Z = Corr(X⊥Z,Y⊥Z)
correlation after residualizing
Controls
ρXY = ρXZρYZ + ρXY·Z√(1−ρXZ²)√(1−ρYZ²).
Geometry checklist
Linear dependence as angle in L²
covariance = centered inner product correlation = normalized slope/angle partial = after removing Z
Raw correlation may be created by common dependence on Z.
Correlation matrix and PSD condition
valid because it is built from residual geometry
Partial correlation geometry
project out Z, then correlate residuals
Interpretation
Correlation measures the angle between centered variables. Partial correlation measures the angle after removing the direction generated by Z.
Covariance.
Positive if centered variables move together; negative if they move in opposite directions.
Correlation.
Scale-free covariance: always between −1 and 1.
PSD matrix.
A covariance matrix is always positive semidefinite because every linear combination has nonnegative variance.
Partial correlation.
Correlation of residuals after projecting X and Y away from Z.
What to notice
If X and Y both depend on Z, their raw correlation may be large even when their residual correlation is zero.
