Variance and total variance

Variance is squared L2 distance from the mean. Total variance splits dispersion into two orthogonal pieces: variation inside groups and variation between group means.
Variance
Var(X)=E[(X−μ)²]
μ=E[X]
L2 geometry
Var(X)=||X−E[X]||²₂
σ=||X−μ||₂
Standardization
Z=(X−μ)/σ
E[Z]=0, Var(Z)=1
Total variance
Var(X)=E[Var(X|G)]
+ Var(E[X|G])
Controls
Var(X)=E[Var(X|G)]+Var(E[X|G]).
Variance logic
Dispersion as squared distance from the mean
global mean μ group means within distances between distances
Each point contributes a squared distance to μ.
Within-between decomposition
variance as two additive pieces
Standardization
translate by μ, rescale by σ
Interpretation
Variance measures how far the random variable is from its constant projection E[X]. Conditioning on a group G decomposes that distance into within-group noise plus between-group structure.
Variance.
Average squared distance from the mean: \(E[(X-\mu)^2]\).
Standard deviation.
Same unit as X: \(\sigma=\sqrt{Var(X)}\).
Within variation.
Noise left after knowing the group: \(E[Var(X|G)]\).
Between variation.
Dispersion of group means: \(Var(E[X|G])\).
What to notice
If groups have similar means, variance is mostly within. If group means are far apart, variance is mostly between. Standardization removes location and scale.
