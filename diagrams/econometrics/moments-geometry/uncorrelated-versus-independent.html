Uncorrelated versus independent

Zero correlation means only linear orthogonality. Independence means full factorization of the joint law. Nonlinear dependence can hide completely from covariance.
Uncorrelated
Cov(X,Y)=0
Corr(X,Y)=0
Independent
P(X∈A,Y∈B)=P(X∈A)P(Y∈B)
all measurable A,B
Implication
X ⟂ Y ⇒ Cov(X,Y)=0
if second moments exist

⇒ independence
Controls

Interpretation
Correlation is a linear diagnostic. Independence is a statement about the whole joint distribution.
Uncorrelated.
Only says \(E[(X-\mu_X)(Y-\mu_Y)]=0\). It is orthogonality in \(L^2\).
Independent.
Every event about X is probabilistically unrelated to every event about Y.
Counterexample.
If \(X\) is symmetric and \(Y=X^2\), then covariance can be zero although \(Y\) is determined by X.
Gaussian exception.
For a jointly normal vector, zero covariance is enough for independence.
What to notice
Look for nonlinear geometry: U-shapes, circles, crosses, or conditional distribution changes. These can have nearly zero correlation and still be dependent.
