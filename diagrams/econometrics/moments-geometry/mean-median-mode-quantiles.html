Mean, median, mode, and quantiles

The four location functionals answer four different geometric questions. The mean balances the distribution, the median splits probability in half, the mode locates peak concentration, and the quantile marks where cumulative mass first reaches a chosen level.
Mean
\(m_2=\arg\min_a E[(X-a)^2]\)
balance / center of mass
Median
\(m_1\in\arg\min_a E|X-a|\)
half the probability on each side
Mode
\(mode\in\arg\max_x f(x)\)
highest local concentration
Quantile
\(Q(\tau)=\inf\{x:F(x)\ge\tau\}\)
mass level marker
Controls
mean = balance; median = half mass; mode = peak; quantile = cumulative threshold.

Interpretation
When the law is symmetric and unimodal, mean, median, and mode are close. Under skewness or contamination they separate because they solve different geometric problems.
Mean.
Balances the whole distribution; very sensitive to tail magnitude.
Median.
Depends on order and cumulative mass; robust to extreme values.
Mode.
Tracks local concentration; may jump in bimodal settings.
Quantile.
Generalizes the median: \(Q(\tau)\) is where the CDF first reaches \(\tau\).
