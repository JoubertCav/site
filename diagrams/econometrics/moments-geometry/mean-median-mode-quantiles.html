Interpretation
The mean, median, mode, and quantiles summarize different geometry. They agree only in special cases, such as symmetric unimodal distributions.
Mean.
Balances mass and uses magnitudes. Sensitive to outliers.
Median.
Uses only order and cumulative mass. Robust to extreme tails.
Mode.
Tracks where density is highest. Good for concentration, not for balance.
Quantile.
Generalizes the median to any probability level Ï„.
What to notice
In a right-skewed law, the mean is pulled to the right tail, the median stays between balance and concentration, and the mode remains near the peak.
