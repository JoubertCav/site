Markov, Chebyshev, Hölder, and Minkowski

Inequalities are geometric safety rails: moments control tail probability; norms control products, sums, and covariance. The point is not sharp prediction, but a guaranteed envelope that works with limited information.

Interpretation
Markov and Chebyshev turn moments into tail guarantees. Hölder and Minkowski turn Lᵖ geometry into product and sum guarantees.
Markov.
For nonnegative variables, large values cannot occupy too much probability mass if the mean is small.
Chebyshev.
Variance controls probability of being far from the mean without assuming normality.
Hölder / covariance.
Products and covariance are controlled by norm lengths.
Minkowski.
The Lᵖ norm obeys triangle geometry: the norm of a sum is no larger than the sum of norms.
What to notice
These inequalities are one-way guarantees. They may be loose, but they are robust because they need very little distributional information.
