Liquidity Trap and the Zero Lower Bound

When money demand becomes nearly horizontal at the zero lower bound, conventional money expansion has little effect on the nominal interest rate. Expectations then become central because expected inflation can lower the real rate even when the nominal rate is stuck.

Money demand and ineffective conventional policy

What to notice

  • In the trap, money demand is almost flat near the lower bound.
  • A higher money supply then changes balances much more than the interest rate.
  • Conventional monetary policy is weak because the nominal rate is already at its floor.
  • Expected inflation lowers the real rate and can stimulate demand even at the ZLB.

Economic interpretation

mechanism

Horizontal demand

Near the ZLB, agents willingly absorb extra money without requiring a much lower interest rate.

Ineffective conventional policy

Open-market purchases mainly raise money holdings, not spending through lower nominal rates.

Expectations

Forward guidance or higher expected inflation can reduce the real rate when the nominal rate is stuck.

Exam-style summary

  • Liquidity trap: money demand becomes highly elastic at very low rates.
  • ZLB: the nominal rate cannot fall below its lower bound.
  • Then increases in M/P have little effect on i.
  • Expectations matter because \( r = i - E\pi \).
