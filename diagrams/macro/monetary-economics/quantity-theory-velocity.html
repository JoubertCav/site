Quantity Theory and Velocity

The equation of exchange links money, velocity, prices, and real output. Inflation appears when nominal spending grows faster than real production.

What to notice

  • Higher money growth raises nominal spending growth.
  • Higher real output growth absorbs nominal spending growth and lowers inflation.
  • Velocity shocks move prices even without a money-stock change.
  • If velocity is stable, π ≈ μ − g.

Economic interpretation

quantity theory

Money M

The stock of means of payment. Faster M growth pushes nominal expenditure upward.

Velocity V

The average frequency with which money turns over in transactions.

Real output Y

The real goods side. If Y grows, the same nominal spending pressure produces less inflation.

Exam-style summary

  • Equation of exchange: MV = PY.
  • Taking logs: log P = log M + log V − log Y.
  • Growth form: π ≈ μ + v − g.
  • Classical quantity theory adds stable V and long-run real-output determination.
