Keynesian Money Demand

Money demand is a portfolio choice: people hold money for transactions, precautionary liquidity, and speculative safety when bonds look risky.

What to notice

  • Income raises the transaction need for money.
  • Uncertainty raises precautionary balances.
  • The interest rate lowers speculative money demand by making bonds more attractive.
  • A higher money supply lowers the equilibrium interest rate, unless demand is extremely interest-elastic.

Economic interpretation

Keynesian liquidity preference

Transactions motive

Higher income means more purchases, payments, and settlement needs.

Precautionary motive

Higher uncertainty makes households and firms hold a liquidity buffer.

Speculative motive

When interest rates are low, agents fear bond capital losses and prefer money.

Exam-style summary

  • Keynesian money demand: L = L(Y,i), increasing in Y and decreasing in i.
  • Transactions and precautionary motives explain the positive income effect.
  • Speculative demand explains the negative interest-rate effect.
  • In the money market, M/P fixed vertically; demand shifts move the equilibrium interest rate.
