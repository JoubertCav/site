Open-Market Operations and Reserve Requirements

A cleaner separation of the two channels: open-market operations change reserves and the monetary base; reserve requirements change the amount of deposits and broad money a given base can support.

What to notice

  • OMO purchases expand reserves; OMO sales drain reserves.
  • The monetary base changes through the central bank balance sheet.
  • Reserve requirements change the multiplier, not the base directly.
  • If rr rises, banks may need more reserves or must contract deposits and loans.

Economic interpretation

classical instruments

Open-market purchase

The central bank buys securities and pays by creating reserves.

Open-market sale

The central bank sells securities and removes reserves from banks.

Reserve requirement

A higher rr makes each deposit require more reserves, lowering m.

Exam-style summary

  • OMO purchase: ΔB > 0 and reserves rise.
  • OMO sale: ΔB < 0 and reserves fall.
  • Multiplier: m = (1+c)/(c+rr+er).
  • rr ↑ ⇒ m ↓ ⇒ supported M falls for a given B.
