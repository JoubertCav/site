What to notice

  • Higher spending raises optimal cash withdrawals and average money balances.
  • Higher interest makes holding cash more expensive, so money demand falls.
  • Lower conversion costs make withdrawals smaller and more frequent.
  • The square-root rule makes money demand less than proportional to income.

Economic interpretation

inventory theory

Transaction cost

Small withdrawals imply many trips to convert bonds into money.

Opportunity cost

Large withdrawals imply high average cash balances and lost interest.

Optimal inventory

The chosen withdrawal size balances fewer trips against more idle cash.

Exam-style summary

  • Baumol–Tobin gives a microfoundation for transactions demand for money.
  • Total cost is bY/C + iC/2.
  • FOC: −bY/C² + i/2 = 0.
  • Therefore C* = √(2bY/i) and Mᵈ* = √(bY/(2i)).
