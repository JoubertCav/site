What to notice

  • B is the monetary base: central-bank money held as currency and reserves.
  • M1 is payment money: currency plus checkable deposits.
  • M2 is broader: M1 plus liquid stores of value.
  • The multiplier falls when reserves are high relative to deposits.

Functions of money

why liquidity matters

Unit of account

Money is the common language of prices, debts, and contracts.

Medium of exchange

Money separates buying from selling and avoids the double coincidence of wants.

Store of value

Money carries purchasing power through time, imperfectly when inflation is high.

Exam-style summary

  • Money aggregates are nested by liquidity, not by “importance.”
  • Central-bank money is not the same as broad money.
  • Deposits make M1 exceed the monetary base when the banking system creates inside money.
  • MV = PY links money to nominal expenditure; it is not by itself a full causal theory.
