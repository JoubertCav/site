What to notice

  • A positive inflation gap pushes the Taylor-rule rate upward.
  • A positive output gap also pushes the policy rate upward.
  • Higher ρ makes policy adjust slowly, even when the rule says rates should move sharply.
  • When φπ > 1, the real rate rises after inflation increases.

Economic interpretation

inflation targeting

Inflation gap

If inflation is above target, the rule prescribes a higher nominal interest rate.

Output gap

If output is above potential, demand pressure calls for tighter policy.

Smoothing

The central bank partly adjusts today and leaves some adjustment for future meetings.

Exam-style summary

  • Taylor rule: iᵀ = r* + π* + φπ(π − π*) + φx x.
  • Inflation targeting means policy reacts to deviations from π*.
  • Taylor principle: φπ > 1 stabilizes inflation in simple New Keynesian logic.
  • Smoothing: i = ρi₋₁ + (1−ρ)iᵀ.
