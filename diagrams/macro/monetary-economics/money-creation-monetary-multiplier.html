Currency leakage and reserve hoarding weaken deposit expansion. A higher c, rr, or er lowers the multiplier.

What to notice

  • Money creation is strongest when currency leakage and reserve ratios are low.
  • Excess reserves act like a brake on the deposit multiplier.
  • Bank-created money is inside money: deposits backed by loans and bank assets.
  • A base injection changes broad money by approximately m × ΔB in this simple model.

Economic interpretation

banking system

Currency leakage

When the public holds more cash per deposit, fewer funds stay inside banks.

Reserve ratios

Required and excess reserves absorb deposits that could otherwise support loans.

Bank lending

Loans create deposits, but the process is constrained by reserves and cash withdrawals.

Exam-style summary

  • With c = C/D and r = rr + er, B = (c+r)D.
  • Since M = C + D = (1+c)D, m = (1+c)/(c+r).
  • Higher c lowers the deposit base and weakens money creation.
  • Higher rr or er raises reserve absorption and lowers m.
