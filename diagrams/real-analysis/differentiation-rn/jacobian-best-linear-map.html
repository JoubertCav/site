Jacobian as best linear map

The Jacobian is the matrix of the total derivative: it is the best local linear map. This app makes the approximation visible through local deformation, column vectors, and the chain rule in \(\mathbb R^n\).

Columns of the Jacobian

The columns of \(J_F(a)\) are the images of the basis vectors \(e_1,e_2\). They generate the linearized local shape.

Local remainder decay

For differentiability, the normalized error \(\|F(a+h)-F(a)-J_F(a)h\|/\|h\|\) tends to \(0\) as \(h\to0\).

differentiability test

Chain rule as composition of linear maps

The derivative of \(G\circ F\) is the composition of the two Jacobians at the right points.

\(J_{G\circ F}=J_GJ_F\)
