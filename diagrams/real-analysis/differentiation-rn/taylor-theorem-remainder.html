contact order
Textbook picture. Keep \(a=0\): \(e^x,\sin x,\cos x,\log(1+x)\), and \((1-x)^{-1}\) use their classical Maclaurin polynomials.
What to watch. Near \(a\), \(T_n\) hugs \(f\). Far from \(a\), the polynomial can diverge from the function.
Meaning. Taylor approximation is local: it copies derivatives at \(a\), not the whole function automatically.
