Completeness and Completion

Completeness is the principle that every Cauchy behavior has a point already inside the space. Completion adds precisely the missing limits, represented by equivalence classes of Cauchy sequences.

What to notice

  • Convergent ⇒ Cauchy in every metric space.
  • Cauchy ⇒ convergent is the extra property called completeness.
  • Completion adds points for missing Cauchy behavior, not for arbitrary sequences.
  • The copy of X inside X̂ is dense through constant sequences.

Exam summary

  • Complete: every Cauchy sequence converges in the space.
  • Completion: a complete metric space X̂ with an isometric dense embedding ι:X→X̂.
  • Construction: X̂ = C(X)/∼, where (xₙ)∼(yₙ) iff d(xₙ,yₙ)→0.
  • Metric: d̂([xₙ],[yₙ]) = limₙ d(xₙ,yₙ).
