06 · compactness in Rⁿ

Heine–Borel theorem

Closed and bounded sets are exactly the compact subsets of finite-dimensional Euclidean space. The app separates the theorem from the two tempting but false half-statements.
The theorem
K ⊂ ℝn is compact  ⇔  K is closed and bounded.
In metric spaces generally, compact ⇒ closed and bounded; the converse is special to ℝn.

Compact exam summary

  • K compact in ℝn ⇒ K closed and bounded.
  • K closed and bounded in ℝn ⇒ K compact. This uses finite-dimensional completeness plus Bolzano–Weierstrass.
  • bounded alone fails: the open ball B(0,1) is bounded but not compact.
  • closed alone fails: ℝn or any closed unbounded ray is closed but not compact.
  • In general metric spaces: closed + bounded need not imply compact.
