What to notice

  • Bounded is one large ball. Totally bounded is finitely many arbitrarily small balls.
  • Total boundedness is stronger than boundedness in general metric spaces.
  • Total boundedness does not imply compactness: missing limits can still occur.
  • Completeness does not imply compactness: the space can be too large to cover by finitely many small balls.

Exam summary

  • ε-net: finite F⊂X such that every x∈X is within ε of some p∈F.
  • Total bounded: such a finite ε-net exists for every ε>0.
  • Metric theorem: compact ⇔ complete + totally bounded.
  • In ℝⁿ: Heine–Borel says compact ⇔ closed and bounded, and bounded sets are totally bounded.
The ε-net picture is finite and visual. The theorem quantifies over every ε>0.
