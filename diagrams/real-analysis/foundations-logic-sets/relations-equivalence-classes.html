Relations and equivalence classes

A binary relation is a region inside U × U. Equivalence relations are exactly the regions whose rows group points into a genuine partition.

Minimal symbolic model
R ⊆ U × U,   xRy ⇔ (x,y) ∈ R.
R is an equivalence relation ⇔
∀x∈U: xRx reflexive
xRy ⇒ yRx symmetric
xRy ∧ yRz ⇒ xRz transitive
[x]R = { y∈U : xRy },   U/R = { [x]R : x∈U }.

The matrix below is the geometry of the relation: filled cells are true statements. Diagonal means reflexivity, mirror symmetry means symmetry, and filled two-step paths force a third filled cell when transitivity holds.

What to notice
  • Reflexivity is the filled diagonal of the matrix.
  • Symmetry is mirror symmetry across the diagonal.
  • Transitivity says every two-step path must have a shortcut.
  • Equivalence classes are not just subsets; together they are a partition.
  • A partition induces an equivalence relation: two points are related iff they lie in the same block.
