Power series and radius of convergence

Coefficient growth determines a symmetric open interval of absolute convergence. Endpoint behavior is a separate one-dimensional series problem.

1. The convergence interval

The radius is centered at c. Inside: absolute convergence. Outside: terms do not tend to zero. At the boundary: retest endpoints.

probe
inside
|x−c| < R

Root test gives absolute convergence.

outside
|x−c| > R

Terms cannot approach 0 fast enough.

boundary
|x−c| = R

Ratio/root tests are usually inconclusive.

5. Termwise operations

Differentiation and integration preserve the same open radius of convergence. Endpoints can change and must be checked again.

same radius

∑ aₙ(x−c)ⁿ

Radius R from coefficient growth.

Same R, because polynomial factors do not change nth-root growth.

C + ∑ aₙ(x−c)ⁿ⁺¹/(n+1)

Same R; endpoints may improve.

What to notice

The radius is not an endpoint theorem.
  • The open interval (c−R,c+R) is decided by coefficient growth.
  • The boundary points are decided by separate series tests.
  • Absolute convergence inside the interval implies ordinary convergence there.
  • Termwise operations keep the same open interval but can alter endpoint behavior.
