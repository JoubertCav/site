Symbolic core

∑aₙ converges ⇔ S_N = ∑ₙ₌₁ᴺ aₙ has a finite limit.
Absolute convergence: ∑|aₙ| < ∞ ⇒ ∑aₙ converges.
Cauchy criterion: ∀ε>0 ∃N: m>n≥N ⇒ |aₙ₊₁+⋯+a_m|<ε.

What to notice

A series is not a curve; it is a sequence of partial sums. The terms may go to zero and the series may still diverge.

Necessary testaₙ ↛ 0 ⇒ ∑aₙ diverges.
Positive seriesconvergence ⇔ partial sums bounded above.
Absolute first∑|aₙ| convergent ⇒ ∑aₙ convergent.
Conditional∑aₙ converges but ∑|aₙ| diverges.

Compact exam-style summary

Partial sumsStudy S_N=∑ₙ₌₁ᴺaₙ. The series converges iff (S_N) converges in ℝ.
Absolute convergenceIf ∑|aₙ| converges, then ∑aₙ converges. If ∑aₙ converges but ∑|aₙ| diverges, convergence is conditional.
ComparisonFor nonnegative terms: 0≤aₙ≤bₙ and ∑bₙ converges ⇒ ∑aₙ converges. If 0≤bₙ≤aₙ and ∑bₙ diverges ⇒ ∑aₙ diverges.
Ratio/rootL<1 gives absolute convergence. L>1 gives divergence. L=1 is inconclusive.
AlternatingIf bₙ↓0, then ∑(-1)ⁿ⁺¹bₙ converges and |S−S_N|≤b_{N+1}.
CondensationIf aₙ≥0 is nonincreasing, then ∑aₙ converges iff ∑2ᵏa_{2ᵏ} converges.
