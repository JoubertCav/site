Ordered fields and the real line

See the real line as an ordered algebraic object: addition translates order, positive multiplication preserves order, negative multiplication reverses order, intervals are order-convex regions, and absolute value is distance.

Important distinction

Ordered-field axioms alone do not imply Archimedeanness. The real line is Archimedean; some ordered fields with infinitesimals are not.

Main diagram

Interpretation

What to notice

The Archimedean property says no positive real step is infinitesimal relative to the line: repeated addition eventually passes any real target.
