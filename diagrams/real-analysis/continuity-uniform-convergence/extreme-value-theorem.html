\(K\subset\mathbb R\)
\[ K\text{ compact},\ f:K\to\mathbb R\text{ continuous} \] \[ \Longrightarrow\ \exists x_{\min},x_{\max}\in K: f(x_{\min})=\min_K f,\quad f(x_{\max})=\max_K f \]
Visual proof. Compact \(K\) ⇒ compact image \(f(K)\). In \(\mathbb R\), compact means the image interval contains its endpoints, so the extrema are attained.

Statement. Continuous \(f:K\to\mathbb R\) with compact \(K\subset\mathbb R^n\) attains both \(\min_K f\) and \(\max_K f\).
Heine–Borel. In \(\mathbb R^n\), compact = closed + bounded. That is why those are the practical hypotheses.
Optimization. A continuous objective on a compact feasible set always has a global minimizer and maximizer.
