Rn topology · continuity as a pullback test

Topological continuity

Open sets live in the target. Continuity asks whether their inverse images remain open in the domain. The diagram below makes this “backward” condition visible in ℝ², the geometric model for ℝⁿ.

continuous map
minimal definition
f : X ⊂ ℝⁿ → Y ⊂ ℝᵐ is continuous
⇔ for every open V ⊂ Y,
f⁻¹(V) is open in X.
Equivalently: closed sets pull back to closed sets.
Metric formFor each a ∈ X: x → a ⇒ f(x) → f(a). In Euclidean spaces this is equivalent to the open-set test.
HomeomorphismA bijection h with h and h⁻¹ continuous. It preserves the whole topology: opens, closeds, boundaries, convergence.
Preimage firstContinuity does not say images of open sets are open. That stronger behavior comes from the inverse map.
Relative topology“Open in X” means U = X ∩ O, with O open in ℝⁿ.

Logical reading

open preimage criterion
V open in Y
⇒ f⁻¹(V) open in X

Continuity is a statement about how target neighborhoods pull back to domain neighborhoods.

closed preimage criterion
C closed in Y
⇒ f⁻¹(C) closed in X

Equivalent because complements commute with preimages: f⁻¹(Y ∖ C) = X ∖ f⁻¹(C).

metric equivalence
∀ε > 0 ∃δ > 0:
∥x − a∥ < δ ⇒ ∥f(x) − f(a)∥ < ε

In ℝⁿ, this pointwise condition is equivalent to the topological one.

homeomorphism
h bijective, h continuous,
h⁻¹ continuous

Then U is open in X iff h(U) is open in Y. The topology is unchanged, only redrawn.

What to notice

  • The continuity test goes backward: target open set V, domain preimage f⁻¹(V).
  • A non-injective continuous map may still pass every preimage test.
  • Images of open sets can fail to be open unless the inverse map is continuous.
  • A discontinuity is exposed by one open set whose preimage is not open.
