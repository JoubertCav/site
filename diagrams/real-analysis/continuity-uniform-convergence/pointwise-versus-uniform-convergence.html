why uniform matters

Continuity

\(x^n\) has continuous terms but a discontinuous pointwise limit.

Integrals

Spikes converge pointwise to \(0\), while area may stay positive.

Derivatives

\(\sin(nx)/n\to0\) uniformly, but derivatives oscillate.

Pointwise. Each fixed \(x\) converges.
Uniform. One \(N\) works for all \(x\).
