Uniform limit theorem

Uniform convergence preserves continuity because the whole graph is close to one continuous \(f_N\). Pointwise convergence can leave a thin escaping layer and produce a discontinuous limit.

Uniform limit theorem. Uniform limits of continuous functions are continuous.
Key estimate. \( |f(x)-f(a)|\le |f-f_N|(x)+|f_N(x)-f_N(a)|+|f_N-f|(a) \).
Pointwise warning. Continuity can fail when the bad region moves or shrinks instead of disappearing uniformly.
