Interpretation

One quantity, many objects. Choosing q on the main graph simultaneously determines price, marginal revenue, elasticity, and markup.
Why qᴹ matters. At the monopoly quantity, MR = MC. Exactly there, the Lerner index equals the inverse of elasticity in magnitude: L = 1/|ε|.
Core intuition. The less elastic demand is at the chosen point, the easier it is for the monopolist to keep price above marginal cost.

What to notice

1
Demand tells you p(q); MR tells you whether increasing output still raises revenue at the margin.
2
Elasticity is high on the left and falls as q moves right toward the choke quantity.
3
The Lerner index is the normalized markup, so it rises when price stays high relative to cost.
4
At qᴹ the four objects become one story: MR=MC, demand is elastic, and L=1/|ε|.
