What to notice

Monopoly is the lower-output benchmark. With one firm, the firm internalizes the full price effect of output and restricts quantity.
Cournot is partially competitive. Each firm internalizes only its own output effect on price, so total output rises with N.
Competition is the limit. As N grows, each firm becomes small, Pᴺ approaches c, and the deadweight-loss wedge shrinks.

Exam-style summary

1
Start from inverse demand P(Q)=a-bQ and constant marginal cost c.
2
For firm i, write πᵢ=[a-b(qᵢ+Q₋ᵢ)-c]qᵢ.
3
The FOC is a-c-bQ₋ᵢ-2bqᵢ=0.
4
Impose symmetry: qᵢ=q and Q₋ᵢ=(N-1)q.
5
Then qᴺ=(a-c)/[b(N+1)], Qᴺ=N(a-c)/[b(N+1)], and Pᴺ=(a+Nc)/(N+1).
