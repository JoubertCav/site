Monopoly choice

Profit maximization geometry

The optimal quantity maximizes the vertical gap between total revenue and total cost. At the maximum, the two total curves have parallel tangents: slope(TR)=MR and slope(TC)=MC.

Interpretation

Global object. Monopoly is a vertical-gap problem: choose q to maximize TR(q)−TC(q).
Local condition. At the largest vertical gap, the total curves stop pulling apart: MR(q)=MC(q).
Welfare distortion. The monopolist stops before D(q)=MC(q), leaving gains from trade unrealized.

Exam-style summary

1
Write TR(q)=(a-bq)q and TC(q)=F+cq+½dq².
2
Then π(q)=TR(q)-TC(q) is strictly concave because π″(q)=−2b−d<0.
3
Interior FOC: a−2bq=c+dq, hence qᴹ=(a−c)/(2b+d).
4
Price is not chosen independently: pᴹ=a−bqᴹ.
5
Efficiency solves D(qᶜ)=MC(qᶜ). Since MR<D for q>0, qᴹ<qᶜ.
