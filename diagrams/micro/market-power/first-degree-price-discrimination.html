Interpretation

Graphically. Perfect discrimination is the entire area between demand and marginal cost up to qᴾᴰ, cut into personalized price slices.
Efficiency. Output expands until the last buyer’s willingness to pay equals marginal cost. No mutually beneficial units are left out.
Distribution. Efficiency is restored, but consumer surplus is extracted by the monopolist.

Exam-style summary

1
Uniform monopoly sells all units at one price. It chooses qᴹ from MR = MC.
2
First-degree price discrimination charges each unit a different price: pᵢ equals that buyer’s willingness to pay.
3
The firm keeps selling until p(q) = MC, so qᴾᴰ = qᴱ.
4
The outcome is efficient, but CSᴾᴰ = 0 and PSᴾᴰ = total surplus.
