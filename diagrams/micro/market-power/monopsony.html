Distortion wedges

Interpretation

Technical core. The firm does not compare the wage directly to MRP when supply is upward sloping. It compares ME to MRP.
Wage distortion. The chosen wage is read from the supply curve at L^M, so the firm pays less than the worker’s marginal revenue product.
Employment distortion. Since ME > w, the equality ME = MRP occurs before the competitive quantity.

What to notice

1
Supply wage: w(L)=a+bL.
2
Marginal expenditure: ME(L)=a+2bL, so it lies above supply.
3
Monopsony chooses L^M from ME=MRP, then sets w^M=w(L^M).
4
Competition chooses L^C from MRP=w. Hence L^M<L^C.
