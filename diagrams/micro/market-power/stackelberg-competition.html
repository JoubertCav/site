Interpretation

Monopoly as anchor. The leader’s optimal commitment equals the monopoly output. The difference is that followers enter afterward and expand total market quantity.
Follower entry. More followers push total output upward, price and Lerner index downward, and consumer surplus upward.
Sequential advantage. With one follower, the leader produces twice as much as the follower. With many followers, the leader still commits to monopoly output, but its monopoly-normalized profit falls because price is disciplined by follower entry.

Exam-style summary

1
Let \(m=N-1\). After \(q_L\), followers play a symmetric Cournot subgame.
2
Follower output is \(q_F(q_L)=(a-c-bq_L)/[b(m+1)]\), truncated at zero.
3
Substitute follower behavior into leader profit and maximize over \(q_L\).
4
The leader chooses \(q_L^S=(a-c)/(2b)=Q^M\).
5
Then \(Q^S=Q^M(2m+1)/(m+1)\), so total output lies above monopoly and approaches the efficient output as follower entry grows.
