Interpretation

Differentiated products. Each firm faces a downward-sloping residual demand because products are not perfect substitutes.
Free entry. Positive short-run profit attracts entry, which lowers each incumbent’s demand intercept from a(N)=A-gN until profit becomes zero.
Excess capacity. In long-run equilibrium the firm operates to the left of minimum average cost, so it could produce more at a lower average cost if it had a larger residual market.
