    Compact exam-style summary

    The minimal toolkit for solving two-good consumer problems.
    • Write the budget set: \(B(p,m)=\{x\in\mathbb R_+^2:p_1x_1+p_2x_2\le m\}\).
    • If preferences are locally nonsatiated, the budget binds: \(p\cdot x^*=m\).
    • If the optimum is smooth and interior, use \(MRS_{12}=p_1/p_2\).
    • If a good is zero, use KKT inequalities rather than forcing tangency.
    • If preferences have kinks or flat indifference curves, use subgradients, corners, or whole faces of optima.
