Manual contract

core model

The principal designs incentives. The agent chooses an action the principal cannot observe.

The essence of the principal–agent model is a tension: the principal wants to expose the agent to incentives in order to induce effort; the agent, if risk-averse, wants insurance. The optimal second-best contract balances these two objectives.

Economic reading

The key intuition is: the outcome is an imperfect signal of effort. Therefore, the principal uses to make the agent internalize part of the gain generated by high effort, but cannot do so without imposing risk on the agent.
